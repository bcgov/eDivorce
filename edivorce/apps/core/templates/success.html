2. Review your responsibilities

When applying for divorce, you and your spouse have certain responsibilities (duties) under section 7.6 of the Divorce Act (Canada). Your responsibilities include:

Take action in best interests of children
You and your spouse may have parenting time or decision-making responsibility for the children of your marriage. You may also have contact with these children under a contact order. This parenting time, decision-making responsibility and contact shall be done in the best interests of the children.

Protect children from conflict
Conflicts may come up during your process of getting your divorce. You and your spouse shall, to the best of your ability, protect children of your marriage from these conflicts.

Resolve matters through family dispute resolution process
Where appropriate, you and your spouse shall try to resolve the matters that may be subject to an order under this Act through a family dispute resolution process. This may include matters to do with parenting time or child support.

Provide complete, accurate and up-to-date information
The information you and your spouse provide for your application for divorce shall be complete, accurate and up-to-date.

Comply with orders
If you and your spouse are subject to an order made under this Act, you and your spouse shall comply with the order until it is no longer in effect.
